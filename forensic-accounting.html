Forensic Accounting

Forensic accounting is the practice of applying accounting expertise to investigate financial discrepancies, fraud, and disputes, often in support of legal proceedings. Unlike traditional accounting, which focuses on accurate reporting and compliance, forensic accounting emphasizes investigation and evidence. Forensic accountants analyze records to trace funds, uncover hidden assets, and identify irregularities that may point to fraud or mismanagement. According to the Association of Certified Fraud Examiners (ACFE), organizations worldwide lose an estimated 5% of their annual revenue to fraud, underscoring the critical role forensic accountants play in protecting businesses and individuals.

Beyond fraud detection, forensic accounting provides clarity in complex financial situations such as divorce settlements, bankruptcy cases, insurance claims, and corporate disputes. These professionals not only prepare detailed reports but also serve as expert witnesses, translating technical financial findings into clear testimony that courts and clients can understand. Their work restores trust and transparency, helping businesses safeguard assets and individuals achieve fair outcomes. In short, forensic accounting is about following the money with diligence and integrity, ensuring that financial truth supports justice
